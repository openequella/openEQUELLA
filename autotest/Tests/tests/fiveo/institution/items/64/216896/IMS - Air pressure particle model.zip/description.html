Start: Air pressure: particle model
 
Title: Air pressure: particle model 
Description: Experiment with some gas trapped in an air chamber. Notice how the gas is made of particles. Investigate the speed and collisions of the air particles. Discover what happens to them as you change the temperature. Observe how this affects the properties of the gas. For example, measure the change in pressure as you heat the gas. This learning object is one in a series of four objects. 
Topics: Air pressure; Gases; Kinetic theory; Temperature 
Keywords: Air pressure, Gases, Kinetic theory, Particle model, Temperature 
Learning area: Science 
Strand: Energy and change; Natural and processed materials 
Concept:  
Audience user level: 5; 6; 7; 8  Audience type: Student 
Age range: 9-14  Audience sector: School 
Key learning objectives:
  • Students describe the particle nature of gases, including the characteristics of the particle motion.
  • Students use a particle model to explain gas pressure.
  • Students describe the effects of temperature changes on particle motion, and explain how this affects pressure.
Educational value:
  • Presents an interactive particle model of a gas for students to use to investigate particle movement.
  • Provides opportunities for students to observe how changes in temperature can affect particles trapped in an air chamber.
  • Contains a series of multiple-choice questions, requiring students to examine aspects of a particle model simulation.
  • Provides structured feedback to student responses.
Student activity: Analysis; Experiment; Interactives; Modelling; Multiple choice questions
